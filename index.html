Day 1

Push Day

Chest Shoulders Triceps
Warm-up — 5 min
Arm circles · Shoulder rolls · Light push-ups · Torso twists
Exercises
Dumbbell Bench Press
4 sets10–12 reps
Lie flat on the bench with a dumbbell in each hand, held at chest level. Elbows should be at about 45° from your body — not flared straight out. Press the dumbbells up until your arms are fully extended, then lower slowly back to chest level with control. Keep your feet flat on the floor, core tight, and a slight natural arch in your lower back. This is your primary chest builder now that you have the bench.
Incline Dumbbell Press
3 sets10–12 reps
Set your bench to a 30–45° incline. Sit back with a dumbbell in each hand at shoulder level. Press straight up overhead until arms are extended, then lower slowly. The incline shifts the focus to the upper portion of your chest, which is often underdeveloped. Don't go too steep — past 45° it becomes more of a shoulder press. Keep the motion smooth and controlled.
Seated Dumbbell Shoulder Press
3 sets10–12 reps
Sit upright on the bench (or set it to 90°) with a dumbbell in each hand at ear/shoulder height, elbows bent at 90° and palms facing forward. Press both dumbbells straight up until arms are nearly extended, then lower slowly back to the start. Seated removes the temptation to use your legs or lean back, making this stricter and safer for your lower back. Core stays braced throughout.
Lateral Raises
3 sets12–15 reps
Stand with a dumbbell in each hand at your sides, palms facing in. With a slight bend in the elbows, raise both arms out to the sides until they reach shoulder height — like you're pouring pitchers of water at the top. Lower slowly. Don't shrug your shoulders up or swing the weight. Use much lighter weight than you think — this isolates a small muscle (medial delt) and form breaks down fast with too much load.
Tricep Overhead Extension
3 sets12 reps
Sit on the bench and hold a single dumbbell with both hands, gripping the top plate. Raise it overhead until your arms are extended. Keeping your upper arms still and pointing straight up, slowly lower the dumbbell behind your head by bending at the elbows. Feel the stretch in the triceps at the bottom, then press back up. Keep your elbows from flaring out excessively — point them forward slightly.
Tricep Kickbacks
3 sets12 reps
Hinge forward at the hips until your torso is roughly parallel to the floor. Hold a dumbbell in each hand, elbows bent at 90° and tucked tight to your sides — upper arms parallel to the floor. Extend your forearms straight back until arms are fully extended, squeezing the tricep at the top. Lower slowly. Only your forearms move — upper arms stay completely pinned. Great finishing move for the triceps after the overhead extension.
Cool-down — 5 min
Doorway chest stretch · Cross-body shoulder stretch · Overhead tricep stretch · Child's pose

Day 1 Tips

Rest 60–90 seconds between sets. For fat loss, keep rest on the shorter end.
The bench press and incline press are your two biggest calorie-burning moves today — don't rush through them.
Stop 1–2 reps before failure on every set. You have 5 days to get through — don't burn out on day one.
Day 2

Pull Day

Back Biceps Rear Delts
Warm-up — 5 min
Torso twists · Arm swings · Shoulder rolls · Light bodyweight rows
Exercises
Dumbbell Bent-Over Row
4 sets10–12 reps
Stand with feet shoulder-width apart, a dumbbell in each hand. Hinge forward at the hips with a flat back until your torso is at about 45°. Let the dumbbells hang with arms straight, then pull both up toward your hips/lower ribcage by driving your elbows back and squeezing your shoulder blades together at the top. Lower slowly. Flat back is non-negotiable — a rounded back under load risks injury.
Single-Arm Dumbbell Row
3 sets10–12 each side
Place one hand and the same-side knee on the bench for support, body parallel to the floor. Hold a dumbbell in your free hand, arm hanging straight down. Pull the dumbbell up toward your hip, leading with your elbow. Let your arm fully extend at the bottom to get a good lat stretch. The bench support means you can use heavier weight and really isolate each side — great for fixing left/right imbalances.
Dumbbell Pullover
3 sets12 reps
Lie across the bench (upper back on the bench, hips dropped and feet flat on the floor) or lengthwise — either works. Hold one dumbbell with both hands above your chest, arms slightly bent. Lower it slowly back over your head toward the floor, feeling a deep stretch through your lats and chest. Pull it back up over your chest. The bench gives you a much greater range of motion than the floor version — you'll really feel the lats open up.
Bicep Curl
3 sets10–12 reps
Stand with a dumbbell in each hand, arms at your sides, palms facing forward. Keeping elbows pinned to your sides, curl both dumbbells up toward your shoulders. Squeeze the biceps hard at the top, then lower over 2–3 seconds — the slow descent is where a lot of the toning work happens. Don't swing your torso or let elbows drift forward. Alternate one arm at a time if that feels more controlled.
Hammer Curl
3 sets10–12 reps
Same starting position as a regular curl but rotate your palms so they face each other — like you're gripping hammer handles. Curl up keeping elbows at your sides. This variation shifts emphasis to the brachialis (underneath the bicep) and the forearm, which adds thickness to the arm. A great complement to standard curls — do these right after while your arms are still warm.
Rear Delt Fly
3 sets12–15 reps
Hinge forward at the hips with a flat back until your torso is nearly parallel to the floor. Hold a light dumbbell in each hand, arms hanging straight down with a slight elbow bend. Raise both arms out to the sides in a wide arc, squeezing your shoulder blades together at the top. Lower slowly. This targets the rear deltoids and upper back — muscles that are chronically weak in most people and key for posture. Use very light weight.
Cool-down — 5 min
Child's pose · Cross-body shoulder stretch · Lat stretch (grab bench) · Doorway chest stretch

Day 2 Tips

The single-arm row lets you go heavier than bent-over rows — use that to your advantage.
For the rear delt fly, lighter is better — form collapses fast with too much weight.
Focus on squeezing the muscle at the top of each pull movement — that mind-muscle connection drives toning results.
Day 3

Legs Day

Glutes Hamstrings Core Knee-Friendly
Warm-up — 5 min
Hip circles · Leg swings · Bodyweight glute bridges · Ankle rolls
Exercises
Romanian Deadlift
4 sets10–12 repsLow knee stress
Stand with feet hip-width apart, dumbbells in front of your thighs, palms facing you. With a soft passive bend in the knees, push your hips back and hinge forward, letting the dumbbells slide down your legs. Keep your back flat and chest up. Lower until you feel a strong hamstring stretch — usually around mid-shin. Drive your hips forward to stand back up. This is a hip hinge, not a squat — the knee bend is minimal and doesn't load the knee joint.
Dumbbell Glute Bridge
4 sets12–15 repsKnee-friendly
Lie on your back with knees bent and feet flat on the floor, hip-width apart. Place a dumbbell across your hips and hold it in place with both hands. Drive through your heels to lift your hips up toward the ceiling, squeezing your glutes hard at the top. Your body should form a straight line from knees to shoulders at the top. Lower slowly and repeat. This is one of the best glute exercises — the knee bend is comfortable and the load goes through the hips, not the knees.
Single-Leg Romanian Deadlift
3 sets10 each sideLow knee stress
Hold a dumbbell in each hand and stand on one leg. Hinge forward at the hips, letting your back leg lift behind you as a counterbalance, while lowering the dumbbells toward the floor. Keep your hips square to the floor — don't let them rotate open. Return to standing by squeezing the glute of the standing leg. This challenges balance, fires up the glute, and really isolates each side. Start light — it's harder than it looks.
Dumbbell Hip Thrust (Bench)
3 sets12–15 repsKnee-friendly
Sit on the floor with your upper back resting against the side edge of the bench, knees bent, feet flat on the floor. Place a dumbbell across your hips. Drive through your heels to thrust your hips up until your body forms a straight line from shoulders to knees. Squeeze the glutes hard at the top and hold for a second. Lower slowly. The bench elevation gives you a greater range of motion than a floor bridge — this is a serious glute developer.
Standing Calf Raise
3 sets15–20 reps
Stand with a dumbbell in each hand, feet hip-width apart. Rise up onto the balls of your feet as high as you can, squeezing the calves at the top. Lower slowly back down — go all the way down for a full stretch if you can. Calves respond well to higher reps and time under tension, so move slowly and deliberately rather than bouncing through these. You can stand on the edge of a step for extra range of motion if available.
Dead Bug
3 sets8–10 each sideZero knee stress
Lie flat on your back with arms extended toward the ceiling and knees bent at 90° above your hips. Press your lower back firmly into the floor and don't let it leave. Slowly lower your right arm overhead while extending your left leg out straight, hovering both just above the floor. Return to start and repeat on the other side. Move deliberately — if your lower back lifts off the floor, you've gone too far. This is a core stability essential that supports all your other lifts.
Cool-down — 5 min
Standing hamstring stretch · Kneeling hip flexor stretch · Pigeon pose · Seated figure-four stretch

Day 3 Tips

Every exercise here avoids loading your knee joint — this is a full leg day that works around your runner's knee.
If anything causes knee discomfort, stop immediately. The glute bridge and hip thrust should feel completely fine.
Really focus on squeezing the glutes at the top of every bridge and thrust — that's where the work happens.
Day 4

Upper Day

Chest Back Shoulders Arms
Warm-up — 5 min
Arm circles · Torso twists · Light rows · Shoulder rolls
Exercises
Dumbbell Bench Press
3 sets10–12 reps
Flat bench, elbows at 45°, press up and lower slowly. Same as Day 1 but one fewer set — this is a balanced upper day, so you're hitting both push and pull rather than going all-in on one. Keep it controlled and focus on the chest squeeze at the top rather than just moving the weight.
Dumbbell Bent-Over Row
3 sets10–12 reps
Paired directly after the bench press to balance pushing with pulling — this keeps your shoulders healthy over the long run. Hinge at the hips with a flat back, pull the dumbbells to your lower ribcage, and squeeze shoulder blades together at the top. Think of the row as the "antidote" to the bench press — equal push and pull volume is what keeps posture solid.
Seated Dumbbell Shoulder Press
3 sets10–12 reps
Sit upright on the bench with dumbbells at shoulder height, elbows at 90°, palms facing forward. Press straight up overhead, then lower slowly. Seated keeps it strict — no leg drive or lower back arch. Your shoulders should be getting more warmed up and strong by this point in the week, so you may find you can handle a bit more weight than Day 1.
Dumbbell Shrugs
3 sets12–15 reps
Stand holding a dumbbell in each hand at your sides. Shrug your shoulders straight up toward your ears as high as possible, hold for a full second at the top, then lower slowly. No rolling — straight up, straight down. Shrugs train the trapezius, which supports your neck, upper back, and posture. A strong upper back is important for both aesthetics and everyday comfort.
Incline Dumbbell Curl
3 sets10–12 reps
Set the bench to a 45–60° incline and sit back against it with a dumbbell in each hand. Let your arms hang straight down behind you — this extended starting position gives the bicep a longer stretch than a standing curl. Curl both dumbbells up, keeping your upper arms vertical and elbows back. The incline position removes the ability to cheat with your body, making this one of the best pure bicep exercises you can do with dumbbells.
Lateral Raises
3 sets12–15 reps
Stand with dumbbells at your sides, palms facing in. Raise both arms out to shoulder height with a slight elbow bend. Lower slowly. Light weight, strict form. By now in the week your delts have had a good amount of work — keep the weight conservative and focus on the burn rather than loading up.
Plank
3 sets40 seconds
Forearms on the floor, elbows under shoulders, body in a straight line from head to heels. Squeeze your glutes, brace your abs, and breathe steadily. Don't let your hips sag or pike up. Slightly longer than Day 1's 30 seconds — your core should be getting stronger by this point in the week. If 40 seconds is easy, push toward 50–60.
Cool-down — 5 min
Doorway chest stretch · Lat stretch · Overhead tricep stretch · Cross-body shoulder stretch

Day 4 Tips

This is a balanced upper day — equal push and pull keeps your shoulder joints healthy over time.
The incline curl is a serious bicep builder — go lighter than you would for standing curls, the stretch position makes it much harder.
You're near the end of the week — stay focused but don't push to failure. Save something for Day 5.
Day 5

Full Body

Everything Compound Fat Burn
Warm-up — 5 min
Jumping jacks · Arm swings · Torso twists · Hip circles · Shoulder rolls
Exercises
Dumbbell Bench Press
3 sets12 reps
Flat bench press to open the session — moderate weight today, slightly higher reps (12) to keep the heart rate up and lean more toward the fat-burning end. Rest only 45–60 seconds after this before moving to the row. The shorter rest is intentional on this day — it's a full body circuit-style approach to maximize calorie burn.
Bent-Over Row
3 sets12 reps
Superset this directly after the bench press with minimal rest between the two — this push/pull superset keeps blood moving and raises your heart rate significantly compared to resting between sets. Hinge forward, flat back, pull to lower ribcage, squeeze shoulder blades. Then rest 60–75 seconds before the next superset pair.
Romanian Deadlift
3 sets12 repsLow knee stress
The biggest lower body movement of the day — hip hinge, flat back, soft knee bend only. This hits hamstrings and glutes hard while being completely knee-joint friendly. On this full body day it acts as your main leg compound. Drive through the hips to stand, squeeze the glutes at the top of each rep. Rest 60 seconds, then move to the shoulder press.
Standing Dumbbell Shoulder Press
3 sets12 reps
Standing today instead of seated — the standing position recruits your core and stabilizer muscles significantly more, adding to the full-body calorie burn of this session. Feet shoulder-width apart, dumbbells at shoulder height, press overhead. Keep your core braced so you're not leaning back. This is one of the best full-body bang-for-your-buck movements you can do.
Dumbbell Glute Bridge
3 sets15 repsKnee-friendly
On your back, dumbbell across your hips, feet flat on the floor. Drive through your heels and squeeze your glutes to lift your hips to a straight line. Hold for a full second at the top, then lower slowly. 15 reps today — slightly higher than Day 3 to push the glute endurance and keep the calorie burn going. This is your second lower body move of the day and a great active recovery piece after the deadlift.
Hammer Curl to Press
3 sets10 reps
Stand with dumbbells at your sides, palms facing each other. Curl both dumbbells up to shoulder height (hammer grip), then rotate your palms forward and press them overhead in one fluid motion. Lower back to shoulders, rotate palms back to facing each other, and lower to the start. This combination move hits your biceps, shoulders, and triceps in a single rep — great for fat loss because it recruits more muscle groups simultaneously and keeps the heart rate elevated.
Plank
3 sets40 seconds
Finish the week strong. Forearms down, elbows under shoulders, body straight from head to heels. Squeeze everything — glutes, abs, quads. Breathe slowly and hold. After 5 days of work, this plank is your victory lap. Push for the full 40 seconds on each set. If you've got more in the tank, go to 50 or 60.
Cool-down — 5 min
Full body stretch: chest · lats · hamstrings · hip flexors · shoulders · lower back

Day 5 Tips

Superset the bench press and row — go straight from one to the other with no rest. This is the key fat-burning technique for today.
Keep rest periods to 45–60 seconds throughout this session — shorter rest = more calories burned.
You made it through the week. Take 1–2 full rest days before starting again. Recovery is where the results actually happen.
