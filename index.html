Push A

Day 1  ·  Chest, Shoulders & Triceps
Chest Shoulders Triceps
Warm-up — 5 min
Arm circles · Shoulder rolls · 10 light push-ups · Torso twists
Exercises
Dumbbell Bench Press
4 sets8–10 reps
Lie flat on the bench with a dumbbell in each hand at chest level, elbows at 45°. Press up until arms are extended, then lower slowly back to chest. Push A uses slightly lower reps (8–10) and heavier weight than Push B — this is your strength-focused push day. Focus on control and a full range of motion, feeling the chest stretch at the bottom of each rep.
Incline Dumbbell Press
4 sets8–10 reps
Bench set to 30–45°. Press dumbbells from shoulder height straight up. Targets the upper chest. Keep elbows at 45° from your body — not flared out. 4 sets here on Push A because upper chest is typically underdeveloped and benefits from extra volume on your heavier day.
Seated Dumbbell Shoulder Press
3 sets10 reps
Sit upright on the bench, dumbbells at ear height, elbows at 90°, palms forward. Press overhead until arms are extended, lower slowly. Seated removes leg drive and lower back arch, making this strict and shoulder-specific. Core braced throughout.
Lateral Raises
3 sets12–15 reps
Stand with dumbbells at sides, palms in. Slight elbow bend. Raise arms out to shoulder height — no shrugging, no swinging. Lower slowly over 3 seconds. Use light weight. The medial deltoid gives the shoulder its width — consistent work here pays off visually over time.
Tricep Overhead Extension
3 sets10–12 reps
Sit on the bench and hold one dumbbell with both hands overhead. Keeping upper arms pointed straight up, lower the dumbbell behind your head by bending at the elbows. Press back up. The long head of the tricep gets its best stretch in this overhead position — it's the largest part of the tricep and this is one of the best ways to develop it.
Tricep Kickbacks
3 sets12 reps
Hinge forward at hips, torso parallel to floor, upper arms pinned to sides and parallel to the floor. Extend forearms straight back until arms are fully extended. Squeeze tricep hard at the top. Lower slowly. Only forearms move. A great finishing burner after the overhead extension — your triceps will be well fatigued by now so go lighter than you might expect.
Cool-down — 5 min
Doorway chest stretch · Cross-body shoulder stretch · Overhead tricep stretch · Child's pose

Push A Tips

This is your heavier push day — 8–10 reps means go heavier than Push B. Add weight when you can do 10 reps cleanly for 2 sessions in a row.
Rest 90 seconds between sets on the bench press and incline press. Drop to 60 seconds on the isolation moves.
Push A and Push B share the same core movements — the difference is rep range and intent. A is strength, B is volume.

Pull A

Day 2  ·  Back, Biceps & Rear Delts
Back Biceps Rear Delts
Warm-up — 5 min
Torso twists · Arm swings · Shoulder rolls · Light band pull-aparts (or no-weight row motion)
Exercises
Dumbbell Bent-Over Row
4 sets8–10 reps
Feet shoulder-width, hinge to 45°, flat back. Pull dumbbells to lower ribcage, drive elbows back, squeeze shoulder blades hard. Lower with control. Pull A is your heavier pull day — go heavier here than on Pull B, 8–10 reps. The row is the back's equivalent of the bench press in terms of importance. Don't rush it.
Single-Arm Dumbbell Row
4 sets8–10 each side
One hand and same-side knee braced on the bench. Dumbbell hangs from the free hand. Pull the dumbbell up toward your hip, leading with the elbow. Let the arm fully extend at the bottom for a full lat stretch. The unilateral nature lets you go heavier than the bilateral row and fixes imbalances between sides. 4 sets on Pull A — this is where you really build the lats.
Dumbbell Pullover
3 sets10–12 reps
Lie lengthwise or across the bench, upper back supported. Hold one dumbbell with both hands above your chest, slight elbow bend. Lower it slowly behind your head until you feel a deep lat and chest stretch, then pull it back up. The bench gives you far greater range than the floor version. This is one of the few exercises that works both chest and lats simultaneously.
Incline Dumbbell Curl
3 sets10 reps
Set bench to 45–60° incline. Sit back and let your arms hang straight down. Curl both dumbbells up, keeping upper arms vertical and elbows back. The incline forces a full stretch at the bottom — the bicep is under tension through a longer range of motion than a standing curl. You will not be able to use as much weight here. That's expected and correct.
Rear Delt Fly
3 sets12–15 reps
Hinge forward until torso is nearly parallel to the floor. Hold light dumbbells hanging down, slight elbow bend. Raise both arms out to the sides in a wide arc, squeezing shoulder blades together at the top. Lower slowly. Rear delts are chronically weak and overlooked — they are critical for shoulder health, posture, and the overall rounded look of the shoulder. Use very light weight and focus on the squeeze.
Dumbbell Shrugs
3 sets12–15 reps
Stand with dumbbells at sides. Shrug shoulders straight up as high as possible. Hold one full second at the top. Lower slowly. Straight up, straight down — no rolling. Traps are a finishing move on Pull days. Strong traps support your neck, upper back, and posture, and are especially important as your rowing volume increases on this program.
Cool-down — 5 min
Child's pose · Cross-body shoulder stretch · Lat stretch against bench · Doorway chest opener

Pull A Tips

Pull A is your heavy back day. The bent-over row and single-arm row are where you should be chasing progressive overload over the weeks.
On the single-arm row, really let the arm hang and stretch fully at the bottom — that eccentric stretch is where a lot of lat development comes from.
Rest 90 sec on the compound rows, 60 sec on curls and isolation work.

Legs A

Day 3  ·  Hamstring & Posterior Chain Focus
Hamstrings Glutes Core Knee-Friendly
Warm-up — 5 min
Hip circles · Leg swings · Bodyweight glute bridges x15 · Ankle rolls
Exercises
Romanian Deadlift
4 sets8–10 repsLow knee load
Stand with dumbbells in front of thighs, feet hip-width. Push hips back and hinge forward, keeping back flat and chest up. Lower until you feel a strong hamstring stretch — usually mid-shin. Drive hips forward to stand. The knee bend is soft and passive — the load goes entirely through the hips and hamstrings, not the knee joint. Legs A uses heavier weight and lower reps — this is your strength deadlift day.
Single-Leg Romanian Deadlift
3 sets8–10 each sideLow knee load
Hold dumbbells in both hands. Balance on one leg with a soft knee bend. Hinge forward at the hips, letting your rear leg rise as a counterbalance. Keep hips square to the floor — don't let them rotate open. Return by squeezing the glute of the standing leg. This single-leg work directly translates to running mechanics and builds the hip stability that protects your knee over time.
Dumbbell Hip Thrust (Bench)
4 sets10–12 repsKnee-friendly
Upper back against the bench edge, knees bent, feet flat on floor. Dumbbell across hips. Drive through heels, thrust hips up to a straight line from shoulders to knees. Squeeze glutes hard at the top and hold one second. Lower with control. The bench elevation increases the range of motion significantly over a floor bridge — this is the best glute builder in the program.
Standing Calf Raise
3 sets15–20 reps
Hold dumbbells at sides, rise onto balls of feet as high as possible. Hold the top for one second. Lower slowly all the way down for a full stretch. Calves respond best to slow, full-range reps rather than fast bouncing. Strong calves reduce impact stress on the knee during running — very relevant for your recovery.
Dead Bug
3 sets8–10 each sideZero knee stress
On your back, arms up toward ceiling, knees at 90° above hips. Lower back pressed firmly into the floor throughout. Slowly lower right arm overhead while extending left leg out, hovering just above the floor. Return and switch sides. Move deliberately — if your lower back lifts, you've gone too far. Core stability built here transfers directly into every other lift in this program.
Cool-down — 5 min
Standing hamstring stretch · Kneeling hip flexor stretch · Pigeon pose · Seated figure-four

Legs A Tips

Legs A is your posterior chain strength day — heavier RDL, more hip thrust sets, focused on building the hamstrings and glutes that protect your knee from the outside in.
Stop any exercise immediately if you feel knee discomfort — not muscle fatigue, but actual knee pain. Everything here is designed to avoid it, but everyone is different.
The single-leg RDL directly trains the balance and hip stability that makes running mechanics safer. It's one of the most PT-relevant exercises in this plan.

Push B

Day 4  ·  Chest, Shoulders & Triceps — Volume Day
Chest Shoulders Triceps
Warm-up — 5 min
Arm circles · Shoulder rolls · 10 push-ups · Chest doorway stretch
Exercises
Dumbbell Bench Press
3 sets12–15 reps
Same movement as Push A but lighter weight, higher reps (12–15). Push B is your volume day — more total reps, less load. This higher rep range increases muscular endurance, drives more blood into the muscle, and contributes more to the toning and fat-loss side of your goals. Focus on a controlled tempo and feeling the chest work rather than moving max weight.
Dumbbell Chest Fly
3 sets12–15 reps
Lie on the flat bench with a dumbbell in each hand, arms extended above your chest, palms facing each other. With a fixed slight bend in the elbows, lower both arms out to the sides in a wide arc until you feel a deep chest stretch. Squeeze the chest to bring them back together above you. This is a stretch-focused isolation move — use light weight. The fly appears on Push B (volume day) rather than A because it's an isolation exercise, better suited to higher reps.
Standing Dumbbell Shoulder Press
3 sets12–15 reps
Standing this time rather than seated — the standing position recruits your core and stabilizers more, adding to total calorie burn. Feet shoulder-width, dumbbells at shoulder height, core braced. Press overhead, lower slowly. Higher reps than Push A — drop the weight accordingly. If your lower back wants to arch, the weight is too heavy.
Lateral Raises
3 sets15 reps
Same movement as Push A. Light weight, arms out to shoulder height, slow lower. On the volume day your delts will be fatigued from the pressing — stick with the same or lighter weight than Push A and chase the burn rather than the load. 15 reps steady.
Tricep Overhead Extension
3 sets12–15 reps
Seated on bench, both hands gripping one dumbbell overhead. Upper arms stay pointing straight up throughout. Lower behind the head, feel the tricep stretch, press back up. More reps than Push A — use slightly lighter weight and focus on the full range of motion and the stretch at the bottom.
Diamond Push-Ups
3 setsMax reps
Get into push-up position but place your hands together under your chest, forming a diamond shape with thumbs and index fingers. Lower your chest toward your hands, then push back up. This hand position places intense emphasis on the triceps and inner chest. Do as many clean reps as you can. Finish Push B with these as a burnout — your triceps will be well warmed up and this will push them to fatigue without any equipment needed.
Cool-down — 5 min
Doorway chest stretch · Overhead tricep stretch · Cross-body shoulder stretch · Child's pose

Push B Tips

Push B is lighter and higher rep than Push A. Drop the weight by roughly 10–15% and focus on controlled movement and feeling the muscle rather than lifting max load.
The chest fly is new here — it's an isolation stretch movement. Keep the elbows fixed in their slight bend and don't go so deep that your shoulders feel stressed.
Rest 60 seconds between all sets on Push B. The shorter rest is intentional — it keeps the metabolic demand high.

Pull B

Day 5  ·  Back, Biceps & Rear Delts — Volume Day
Back Biceps Rear Delts
Warm-up — 5 min
Torso twists · Arm circles · Shoulder rolls · Light face-pulls motion
Exercises
Dumbbell Bent-Over Row
3 sets12–15 reps
Same hinge, same flat back, same elbow drive — but lighter weight and 12–15 reps today. Pull B is your volume back day. The extra reps drive more blood into the lats and rhomboids and contribute more to the endurance and toning side of your goals. Take 60 seconds rest between sets rather than 90.
Chest-Supported Row
3 sets12–15 reps
Set the bench to a low incline (30°) and lie face-down against it with your chest on the pad. Hold a dumbbell in each hand, arms hanging straight down. Row both dumbbells up toward your hips, squeezing your shoulder blades together. Lower with control. The bench supports your torso, completely removing any lower back fatigue — this means you can focus 100% on the back muscles with no compensating. Great variation to alternate with the standard bent-over row.
Bicep Curl
3 sets12–15 reps
Standing, palms forward, elbows pinned to sides. Curl up, squeeze at the top, lower slowly over 3 seconds. The slow descent (eccentric) is where a significant amount of the toning work happens — don't drop the weights down. 12–15 reps on Pull B rather than 10 on Pull A. Same motion, lighter weight, more reps.
Hammer Curl
3 sets12–15 reps
Palms facing each other, curl up keeping elbows at sides. Targets the brachialis and forearm alongside the bicep. Superset these directly after the standard curls with 15 seconds rest between — your biceps will be burning. This curl/hammer curl pairing is one of the most effective arm-toning combinations in the program.
Rear Delt Fly
3 sets15 reps
Hinge forward, light dumbbells hanging. Raise arms wide to the sides, squeeze shoulder blades at the top. Lower slowly. 15 reps today — this is a toning-focused, high-rep set. The rear delts respond well to volume and are very difficult to overtrain. If anything in the program you can push the reps here, this is it.
Dumbbell Pullover
3 sets12–15 reps
Upper back on the bench, both hands gripping one dumbbell. Lower it behind your head slowly, feel the deep lat and chest stretch, pull back up over your chest. More reps than Pull A — lighter weight, focus on the full stretch. A great finisher on Pull B that leaves the lats under tension for longer total time across the rep.
Cool-down — 5 min
Child's pose · Lat stretch · Cross-body shoulder stretch · Neck rolls

Pull B Tips

The chest-supported row is new here — the bench takes all lower back fatigue out of the equation. Take advantage by really focusing on squeezing the shoulder blades together.
Superset the bicep curl and hammer curl back-to-back — it's a brutal but effective combination for arm toning.
Rest 60 seconds between sets throughout Pull B. Keep the pace moving.

Legs B

Day 6  ·  Glute, Quad Intro & Running Strength
Glutes Quads (intro) Core PT clearance for BSS
⚠ Note on Bulgarian Split Squat: This exercise involves controlled knee bend and should only be attempted once your PT has given the go-ahead. Start with bodyweight only, rear foot on the floor (not elevated). Skip it entirely until you have clearance — everything else in this day is safe to do now.
Warm-up — 5 min
Hip circles · Leg swings · Bodyweight glute bridges x15 · Light walking lunges (skip if knee is tender)
Exercises
Dumbbell Glute Bridge
4 sets12–15 repsKnee-friendly
On your back, knees bent, feet flat, dumbbell across your hips. Drive through your heels to lift your hips up until your body forms a straight line from knees to shoulders. Squeeze the glutes hard at the top — hold one full second. Lower slowly. Legs B opens with the glute bridge as its primary compound movement. 4 sets here because glute strength is the cornerstone of runner's knee recovery and prevention.
Romanian Deadlift
3 sets12–15 repsLow knee load
Same hip hinge as Legs A, but lighter weight and more reps today — this is the volume version. 12–15 reps, focus on the hamstring stretch at the bottom and the hip drive at the top. By now in the week your posterior chain has been worked hard — be honest about the weight you pick here.
Bulgarian Split Squat
2 sets8 each sidePT clearance first
⚠ Get PT clearance before attempting. Start with bodyweight only — no dumbbells — for at least 2 weeks. Stand about 2 feet in front of a bench. Place the top of your rear foot flat on the floor behind you (not on the bench yet). Lower your back knee toward the floor by bending your front knee, keeping your front knee tracking over your toes. Return to standing by driving through the front heel. Only 2 sets of 8 to start — this is an introduction, not a working set. The BSS builds the quad strength that stabilizes the patella and directly strengthens the structures involved in runner's knee recovery.
Dumbbell Hip Thrust (Bench)
3 sets15 repsKnee-friendly
Upper back against bench edge, dumbbell across hips, feet flat. Drive hips up until you're in a straight line, squeeze glutes hard for one second at the top, lower slowly. Higher reps today (15) than Legs A — this is the volume version of the hip thrust. By the end of the third set your glutes will be very fatigued. That is the goal.
Standing Calf Raise
3 sets20 reps
Dumbbells at sides, rise onto balls of feet, hold at top, lower fully. 20 reps today — higher than Legs A. Calves work twice per week in this program since they're involved in every running stride. Strong calves reduce the impact load that travels up into the knee. Slow and controlled, full range of motion on every rep.
Plank
3 sets45 secondsZero knee stress
Forearms down, elbows under shoulders, body straight from head to heels. Squeeze everything — glutes, abs, quads. Breathe slowly and hold. 45 seconds to finish the week. This is your end-of-week victory lap. Core strength built here stabilizes your pelvis during running, which directly reduces the mechanical stress that causes runner's knee. It earns its place here every week.
Cool-down — 5 min
Full lower body stretch: hamstrings · hip flexors · glutes · calves · lower back

Legs B Tips

Do not attempt the Bulgarian Split Squat until your PT clears it. When you do start, bodyweight only for the first two weeks minimum — the movement pattern itself is the challenge at first.
Legs B is your glute-volume day. The bridge and hip thrust are where you chase reps rather than weight today.
Take tomorrow completely off. Full rest. Your muscles grow and repair on rest days, not training days — Day 7 is as important as any workout day.
